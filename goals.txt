Great! Here are 5 high-impact features that would significantly enhance the GM dashboard and overall gameplay experience:

1. Initiative Tracker with Turn Management
Drag-and-drop interface to reorder player turns
"Next Turn" button that highlights the current player's character sheet
Visual indicator on player sheets showing "Your Turn!"
Persist initiative order across sessions
Add NPCs/monsters to the initiative tracker
2. GM Combat Tools
Quick damage dealer: click a player card to apply damage/healing
Temporary conditions/status effects (stunned, prone, inspired, etc.)
Condition timers that count down each turn
Broadcast messages to all players ("Roll for initiative!", "Intermission")
Dice roller visible to all players
3. Session History & Notes
Campaign log with timestamped events (character joined, health reached 0, major milestones)
GM notes panel that persists across sessions
Export session summary at end of game
Track XP/level progression over multiple sessions
Character change history (level ups, equipment acquired)
4. Visual Battlefield/Grid System
Simple token placement on gridded map
Upload background images for encounter locations
Move player tokens around (GM control or player drag)
Fog of war for exploration
Distance/range calculator between tokens
5. Encounter Builder & Monster Tracking
Add NPC/enemy combatants with their own health pools
Pre-built encounter templates
Quick stat blocks for enemies visible only to GM
Track multiple enemy groups simultaneously
"Defeat All" button to clear encounter
Bonus Quick Wins:

Dark mode toggle for late-night sessions
Audio notifications when player health is critical
Session timer showing total play time
Player ready/not-ready indicators before starting
Quick reference panel for common rules/actions
Which of these sounds most useful for your tabletop sessions? I'd recommend starting with #1 (Initiative Tracker) since it directly builds on what you have and is essential for combat-heavy games.
